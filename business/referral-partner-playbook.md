## Daily Partner Routine

Do this for 30-45 minutes per day.

1. Pick one category and one city.
2. Find 10 good leads on Instagram, Google Maps, Facebook, or local groups.
3. Add each lead to `lead-tracker.csv`.
4. Send 5-10 personal messages.
5. Follow up with yesterday's warm leads.
6. Save screenshots or notes only if they help remember the conversation.

## Qualification Checklist

Contact the lead if at least two are true:

- Their audience includes families, children, pets, residents, donors, or local shoppers.
- They post events, seasonal content, family photos, parties, or promotions.
- They are in Miami, Doral, Hialeah, Kendall, Sweetwater, Miami Lakes, Coral Gables, or nearby.
- They have a visible DM button, phone, email, website form, or booking link.
- A Santa visit would help them create photos, attendance, donations, or customer goodwill.

Skip for now if:

- They have no recent posts.
- Their audience is not family, local, event, children, pets, or community related.
- They are far outside the service area.
- There is no clear contact path.

## Partner Offer Structures

Keep the offer simple until there is real interest.

- Flat event booking: partner books Santa for a fixed window.
- Referral partner: partner sends the lead, Miami Papa Noel handles booking.
- Shared promo: both accounts post the event or offer.
- Sponsor visit: a business pays for Santa to support a toy drive or nonprofit moment.

Do not promise exclusivity, discounts, commissions, or a referral fee until the family agrees on the exact arrangement in writing.

## What To Ask When They Reply

- What type of event or partnership are you imagining?
- What city or neighborhood?
- What date or date range?
- How many families, children, guests, or pets?
- Will there be a photo setup or backdrop?
- Who handles guest sign-ups, photos, and payments?
- Do you want a flat booking, referral arrangement, or sponsor-supported event?
